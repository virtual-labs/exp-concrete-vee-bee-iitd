### Theory

- The Vee Bee test is another test for determining concrete's workability suitable for concrete with low workability that uses vibration to change the shape of concrete and measures how much effort it takes to remould it. The time it takes to remould the concrete is called **'Vee Bee time.'**

- **IS 10510 1983** talks about the apparatus specifications and **IS 1199 2018** talks about the procedure of determining the workability using this test.

- This test provides insights into the mobility and compatibility of concrete, offering a relative effort measurement as concrete transforms from a defined conical shape to a cylindrical shape through vibration process.

- However, for concrete with a slump value exceeding **50 mm**, the remoulding process occurs so rapidly that the time measurement becomes impractical.

- Moreover, this test is advantageous as compared to previous tests in the sense that the treatment given is similar to actual treatment provided in the field or on site.

### APPARATUS SPECIFICATIONS

The assembly of the apparatus & essential dimensions are shown in the figure, the apparatus shall mainly consist of the following-

**a)** Slump cone without foot rests,

**b)** Mould (shall be made of galvanized iron sheet or such other material which does not get corroded by cement),

**c)** Vibrating table resting on elastic supports (shall be made of cast iron or fabricated by welding a mild steel plate to a suitable steel frame),

**d)** Swivel arm with the hopper

**e)** Contact plastic disc with graduated rod (a moving assembly comprising rod, disc and mass weighs 2750 ± 50 g).

Except this, a tamping rod and a stopwatch is also needed.
